The Common Edition: New Testament is an edition of the New Testament published in 1999; it is standardized edition made to reflect the common word and punctuation choices of translations most frequently used in English speaking churches.
As stated in its introduction, the purpose is a text that can be publicly read before audiences who follow in multiple translations.  As part of the design, the edition is iambic in rhythm, on a sixth grade reading level, and with a formal equivalence level comparable to the English Standard Version 2001. The Common Edition was edited by T.E. Clontz.
